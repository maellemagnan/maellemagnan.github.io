Exploring Gas Kinematics in Barred Galaxies

University of Toronto / Supervisor: Dr. Tobias Géron and Prof. Maria Drout

Date: May 2025 – Present

Stellar bars are elongated features in disk galaxies that torque and funnel gas inward, which can either trigger starbursts or dynamically heat gas—both pathways that can lead to quenching. While strong bars are known to induce rapid inflow, the role of weaker bars remains unclear. I am analyzing spectroscopic observations of 18 galaxies (7 strongly barred, 7 weakly barred, 4 unbarred controls) drawn from Galaxy Zoo DESI (0.01 < z < 0.05, Mr < −18.96), using the Isaac Newton Telescope’s IDS spectrograph with parallel and perpendicular slit alignments. Gaussian fit analysis of Hα emission measures spatial profiles of flux amplitude, line width, and central wavelength. Preliminary results reveal pronounced Hα amplitude peaks at bar ends in strong bars, indicating bar-driven inflow as shown in previous studies (Géron et al., 2024). Similar features were not observed in weakly barred galaxies, indicating that these bars may not cause inflow on the same scale. Ongoing work will convert the fit parameters to physical inflow rates, velocity dispersion, and equivalent widths to assess the efficiency of gas funnelling across bar strengths and their impact on galaxy evolution.
