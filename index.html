About Me

About Me

Hi, I’m Maëlle Magnan, an undergraduate student at the University of Toronto pursuing a degree in Astronomy and Physics with a minor in Mathematics. Growing up in Regina, Saskatchewan, I was lucky to have clear views of the night sky which sparked my passion for the stars. I believe everyone should have this experience which is why I am currently working on a side project quantifying sattelite pollution in astronomical images over the past 10 years.

I currently serve as the Vice President of Communications for the Astronomy Union and act as an advisor for U of T’s Collective Undergraduate Research Experience (CURE). I am also a part of the French and Francophone Association as the Director of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion.

My research interests lie in both small-scale structures, such as stars and galaxies, and their formation and evolutionary processes, as well as in large-scale systems including black holes and the plasma dynamics in their surrounding environments.

Outside of academics, I enjoy hiking, cooking, and playing roller derby. I’ve played competitive roller derby since 2017!
